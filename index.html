What You Need to Know About Trustroots but Haven't Been Told

TL;DR

Trustroots Foundation was dissolved on 24 May 2022. Since July 2022, Trustroots has been operated by 3 private people, 2 of them resigned board members. Community members have been intentionally kept in dark about this by people in control.

On 25 January 2024, almost all volunteers were kicked out from Trustroots Github organization.

The people in control write about decentralization, but silence dissent, perform censorship and concentrate control in their own hands.

What can I do?

  • Tell your friends and contacts on Trustroots about what's going on
  • Join us in Trustroots Matrix channels
  • Write message to the people in control via Trustroots
  • Open issues and comment on GitHub if you don't mind getting banned
  • Unstar Trustroots GitHub repository (393 stars on 2024-03-10)
  • edit your Trustroots profile, and add information and link to this website
  • Warn potential volunteers that the project is not a safe space to contribute at the moment
  • Subscribe for email updates (coming soon)

Details

On January 25, 2024, many volunteers were kicked out of Trustroots github organization, some were completely banned. Also, hundreds of issues and pull requests were closed. This was a culmination of 1.5 years of takeover by a small group of people, after the Trustroots Foundation had been dissolved, and the main founder and developer had stopped being active.

What can be done legally?

  • Callum's claim that he has had full support of Foundation can be verified or debunked
  • If they indeed delegated their authority, last Foundation directors can withdraw it again according to Foundation articles
  • Regarding GDPR, you can exercise your rights - from withdrawing consent to filing a complaint with GDPR authorities e.g. for lack of transparency about changes in data control. The latter can be done e.g. in Germany, where Trustroots server resides (Hetzner Digital).
